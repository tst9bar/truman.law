Truman came to the Bar after seven years in anti-money laundering and regulatory compliance within the banking sector, specialising in transaction review and policy formulation. He formerly worked as a financial crime investigator in a leading European investment and institutional banking group.

Truman joined the Chambers of Lawrence Lok QC SC upon completion of pupillage with Mr Edwin Choy SC, Mr Kevin Chan, Mr Jon Wong and Mr Simon Wong. He has since appeared as sole advocate in the Magistrates’ Courts, the District Court, the Court of First Instance and the Court of Appeal.

His practice encompasses both criminal and civil work. In criminal matters, he is particularly well placed to act in cases involving money laundering, financial misconduct and proceedings connected with the Securities and Futures Commission, drawing upon his prior experience in banking compliance and financial investigation. He has also been engaged in cases involving the gravest forms of offending, including murder and drug trafficking, as well as sexual offences ranging from rape to voyeurism.

On the civil side, Truman focuses on matrimonial and family law, personal injuries and company disputes. His specialist background in transaction monitoring places him in a strong position to assist clients in fund tracing and asset recovery. He also advises private clients and family offices on banking-related and regulatory matters, including enhanced due diligence, letters of no consent, sanctions and cross-border movement of funds.

Truman is proficient in advocacy and written work in both English and Cantonese. He is also a native speaker of Mandarin and Bahasa.

Outside practice, Truman maintains a strong interest in legal writing and scholarship. He is the author of the annotated Family Procedure Ordinance (Cap. 646) and the annotated Fixed Penalty (Smoking Offences) Ordinance (Cap. 600). He contributes legal commentaries on occasion.
